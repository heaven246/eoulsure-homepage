About

From a garage project
to a full-scale mobility team.

Since 1993, EOULSURE has grown from a student car-building club into a multidisciplinary team covering engineering, operations, sponsorship, education, and public outreach.

Who we are

EOULSURE is Korea University's Racing Team. Our members work across mechanical, electrical, control, business, and communication roles to turn ideas into running vehicles.

What we do

We build vehicles, run engineering education, host industry lectures, collaborate with sponsors, and create opportunities for students to experience real-world product development.

Based at Korea University EOULSURE represents student engineering, racing culture, and hands-on mobility projects at Korea University.

01External Relations

The External Relations division secures the budget, infrastructure, and partnerships that make the project possible.

FundingSecures internal and external funding opportunities for vehicle development.
Brand & OperationsManages team promotion, events, public communication, and project operations.
SponsorshipConnects with companies and manages sponsor relationships, parts support, and technical collaborations.

02Electrical Systems

The Electrical Systems division designs and integrates the vehicle's high-voltage and low-voltage electrical architecture.

BatteryDevelops a 300V-class accumulator pack based on high-voltage systems above 60V.
Circuit DesignBuilds safety shutdown circuits, charging circuits, and custom control PCBs.
System IntegrationConnects electrical components and verifies control, safety, and reliability logic.

03Chassis

The Chassis division defines the vehicle's performance, stability, and mechanical integrity. It includes suspension, braking, steering, frame, aero, and powertrain.

SuspensionDesigns suspension geometry to absorb road inputs and maintain tire contact.
BrakesDevelops braking systems for stable deceleration and stopping performance.
SteeringDesigns steering geometry and mechanisms for precise driver input.
FrameCreates the vehicle structure and mounting points for major components.
AerodynamicsImproves airflow, efficiency, and high-speed stability.
PowertrainTransfers motor or drivetrain power to the wheels efficiently and reliably.
